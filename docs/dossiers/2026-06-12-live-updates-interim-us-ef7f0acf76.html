Consensus Facts

  • The US and Iran are edging toward an interim deal that would extend their ceasefire, reopen the Strait of Hormuz, and create a framework for further negotiations on Iran's nuclear program — reported in the body text of both CNN and The Business Times (sourcing Bloomberg).
  • The agreement is described as a memorandum of understanding (MOU); both CNN and The Business Times report the text has been largely agreed but has not yet received final signoff.
  • Geneva, Switzerland, is the most likely location for a signing ceremony, which could take place as early as Sunday — reported by both CNN and The Business Times.
  • Iran's government has not confirmed a final deal; both CNN and The Business Times quote Iranian officials saying no conclusion has been reached, though progress has been made.
  • President Donald Trump announced Thursday that the US had 'ended the war with Iran' and referenced a 'very strong memorandum of understanding,' per both CNN and The Business Times.
  • The interim deal would include an approximately 60-day extended ceasefire period for further negotiations — reported by both CNN and The Business Times.
  • Oil prices fell and equities rose following Trump's Thursday announcement — reported by The Business Times; CNN references the pattern of markets reacting to Trump's deal signals.
  • Israel is pressing the US on the terms of any deal and is skeptical it will lead to a final agreement — reported by both CNN and The Business Times.
  • Trump indicated Vice President JD Vance and envoy Steve Witkoff would represent the US at any signing — reported by The Business Times; CNN references ongoing US military and security planning for weekend talks.
  • The deal's finalization requires approval from Iran's Supreme Leader (identified as Mojtaba Khamenei by The Business Times); both CNN and The Business Times note communication difficulties with Iran's leadership.
