Consensus Facts

  • Torrential rains caused flash floods in West Virginia that inundated homes and businesses, washed out bridges, and led to numerous water rescues (ABC News, CBS News).
  • Gov. Patrick Morrisey declared a state of emergency for all 55 counties Tuesday night and deployed National Guard members to the hardest-hit areas in the northern part of the state (ABC News, CBS News).
  • Forecasters said up to 7 inches of rain fell in some places (ABC News, CBS News).
  • Emergency crews reported dozens of rescues of people stuck in submerged vehicles, but some rescuers were unable to reach locations cut off by floodwaters (ABC News, CBS News).
  • Video and photos on social media showed submerged vehicles in parking lots, including at a Walmart in Weston where muddy water surged into the store, with a nearby hotel's main/first floor inundated (ABC News, CBS News).
  • Aerial views showed extensive flooding in Buckhannon, West Virginia, on Wednesday, July 22, 2026 (Associated Press, CBS News).
  • The northern West Virginia communities of Lewis and Upshur counties were among the hardest hit (Associated Press, CBS News).
