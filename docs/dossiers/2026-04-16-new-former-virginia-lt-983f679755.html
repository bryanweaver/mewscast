Consensus Facts

  • Former Virginia Lt. Gov. Justin Fairfax shot and killed his wife, Cerina Fairfax, and then killed himself in their Annandale, Virginia, home in what police described as a murder-suicide.
  • The shooting occurred just after midnight on Thursday, April 16, 2026.
  • Both of the couple's teenage children were home at the time of the shooting.
  • The couple's teenage son called 911 to report the incident.
  • Fairfax shot his wife multiple times in the basement of the home, then went upstairs and shot himself.
  • Fairfax County Police Chief Kevin Davis described the incident as stemming from an ongoing domestic dispute and a 'complicated or messy divorce.'
  • The couple was separated but still living together in the same home.
  • Fairfax served as Virginia's lieutenant governor from 2018 to 2022 under Democratic Gov. Ralph Northam.
  • In 2019, two women accused Fairfax of sexual assault; he denied the allegations.
  • Fairfax ran for the Democratic gubernatorial nomination in 2021 and lost, finishing fourth in the primary.
  • Former Gov. Ralph Northam issued a statement saying he and his wife Pam were 'devastated by this heartbreaking news.'
  • Davis described the situation as 'a fall from grace for a relatively high-profile family that seemingly had a lot of things going in their favor.'
  • Fairfax was 47 years old at the time of his death.
  • In January, Fairfax called police alleging his wife had assaulted him; police determined no assault had occurred.
  • Fairfax had been recently served paperwork related to upcoming court proceedings in the divorce.
