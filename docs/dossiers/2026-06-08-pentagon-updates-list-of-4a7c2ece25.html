Consensus Facts

  • The Pentagon released a list of 31 recognized religious affiliation codes for service members, reduced from more than 200 previously used codes.
  • The original list designated 21 faiths as variations of Christianity but did not classify The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints as Christian.
  • Republican Sen. Mike Lee of Utah, a member of the LDS Church, publicly criticized the exclusion, calling it 'offensive' and 'repugnant.'
  • Republican Rep. Mike Kennedy of Utah also called the Pentagon's list 'wrong' and urged correction.
  • Republican Sen. John Curtis of Utah called the classification 'unacceptable' and said he was working to ensure a correction.
  • The Pentagon released an updated version of the list on Monday that removed the 'Christian' identifier from the various denominations rather than adding it to LDS.
  • Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth announced the reduction in religious codes, calling the previous system of 200+ codes 'impractical and unusable.'
  • Pentagon spokesman Sean Parnell defended the original policy as logistical, saying it was not designed to make claims on the legitimacy of any faith.
  • The Pentagon's rapid response account on X stated: 'The Pentagon's job is not to adjudicate theological debates, but instead to ensure sincerely-held faith is respected and encouraged in our ranks.'
