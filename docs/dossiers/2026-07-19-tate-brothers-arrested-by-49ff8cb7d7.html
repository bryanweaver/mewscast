Consensus Facts

  • Andrew Tate and his brother Tristan Tate were arrested Saturday in Miami by the U.S. Marshals Service.
  • The arrests were made on a sealed warrant pursuant to extradition proceedings, per the U.S. Marshals Service and Department of Justice.
  • The U.K.'s Crown Prosecution Service (CPS) announced new charges and said it will seek the brothers' extradition to the U.K.
  • Andrew Tate, 39, faces additional charges including seven counts of rape, three counts of arranging or facilitating trafficking for sexual exploitation, three counts of assault occasioning actual bodily harm, and 19 additional charges relating to indecent images of a child and extreme pornography.
  • Tristan Tate, 38, faces one count of sexual assault, two counts of rape, and three counts of arranging or facilitating trafficking for sexual exploitation.
  • The new charging decisions followed receipt of a further file of evidence from Bedfordshire Police and bring the total number of alleged victims in the case to seven.
  • The new charges relate to four additional alleged victims beyond the three previously connected to earlier charges.
  • CPS official Malcolm McHaffie, head of the Special Crime Division, made the statement announcing the charging decisions.
  • The alleged offenses are said to have taken place between July 2010 and August 2017.
  • The Tate brothers are dual U.S. and British citizens who moved to Romania in 2016 and were arrested there in 2022 on allegations related to sexual exploitation schemes.
  • The brothers have consistently denied all allegations and have not been convicted of the alleged crimes.
  • Attorney Joseph McBride, representing the Tates, characterized the arrests as a 'political hit' and an 'egregious abuse' of authority, asserting the brothers would 'walk free.'
  • The brothers are former kickboxers known for social media content promoting wealth and misogyny, associated with the 'manosphere.'
