Consensus Facts

  • A federal jury in Oakland, California, on Monday unanimously found Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, and OpenAI not liable on all claims in Elon Musk's lawsuit.
  • The jury reached its verdict in less than two hours of deliberation.
  • The jury found that Musk's claims were barred by the statute of limitations — he waited too long to sue.
  • The jury also rejected Musk's claim that Microsoft aided and abetted Altman and Brockman in breach of charitable trust, on the same statute of limitations grounds.
  • The case was overseen by U.S. District Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers, who accepted the jury's advisory verdict and indicated she agreed with its findings.
  • Musk's lead counsel Steven Molo reserved Musk's right to appeal.
  • The trial lasted approximately three weeks in a federal courtroom in Oakland.
  • Musk co-founded OpenAI with Altman in 2015 and first filed his lawsuit in 2024.
  • Musk's core allegation was that Altman and Brockman betrayed OpenAI's nonprofit origins by creating a for-profit arm and enriching themselves.
  • Musk contributed approximately $38 million to OpenAI in its early days.
  • OpenAI's defense argued Musk was motivated by competitive rivalry through his own AI startup, xAI, and that he was always aware of for-profit plans.
  • Six tech billionaires testified during the trial, including Musk, Altman, Brockman, and Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella.
  • Lawyers for OpenAI and Microsoft celebrated with hugs and back-slaps after the verdict was read.
  • None of the principal tech billionaires was in the courtroom when the verdict was read (reported by NBC News; corroborated by no outlet stating any were present).
  • The jury was an advisory jury; Judge Gonzalez Rogers held ultimate legal authority over the outcome.
