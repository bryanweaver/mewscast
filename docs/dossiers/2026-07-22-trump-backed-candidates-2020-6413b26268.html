Consensus Facts

  • Rep. Andy Biggs won the Republican primary for Arizona governor.
  • Biggs was endorsed by President Trump and is a former chair/leader of the House Freedom Caucus.
  • Biggs beat a more moderate Republican, Rep. David Schweikert, among others in the primary.
  • Biggs will face incumbent Democratic Gov. Katie Hobbs in the November general election.
  • Hobbs won her 2022 election against Kari Lake by less than 1 percentage point.
  • The Cook Political Report shifted the Arizona governor's race from 'toss-up' toward a Democratic advantage/'lean Democrat' following the primary.
  • Biggs voted against / refused to certify the 2020 presidential election results, tied to Trump's unverified claims the election was stolen.
  • Alexander (Alex) Kolodin won the Republican nomination for secretary of state and will face incumbent Democrat Adrian Fontes in November.
  • Kolodin is a state lawmaker and attorney who participated in lawsuits challenging the 2020 election results.
  • Trump-endorsed former NFL kicker Jay Feely won the GOP nomination in Arizona's 1st Congressional District, a competitive open seat.
  • The 1st District seat is open because current Rep. David Schweikert ran for governor instead of reelection.
  • Arizona's 6th Congressional District will feature Republican Rep. Juan Ciscomani versus Democrat JoAnna Mendoza, both of whom ran unopposed in their primaries.
  • Ciscomani was first elected in 2022 and won reelection in 2024 with 50% of the vote; Mendoza is a military veteran who served two tours in Iraq and one in Afghanistan.
  • Democratic Rep. Greg Stanton fended off a progressive primary challenge from Kai Newkirk in the blue-leaning 4th Congressional District.
  • Trump won Arizona in 2024, but the state has elected Democrats to statewide offices in recent cycles.
  • Arizona has two U.S. House seats considered true toss-ups among the 18 nationally rated by the Cook Political Report.
