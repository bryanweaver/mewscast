Consensus Facts

  • Smoke from wildfires burning in Canada (and northern Minnesota) drifted into the US midwest, mid-Atlantic and north-east on Friday, July 17, 2026, triggering air quality alerts across multiple states.
  • As of Friday, Detroit, Chicago and Washington DC ranked among the world's most polluted major cities, with Detroit's air quality registering as the worst of any major city in the world.
  • New York City had been blanketed by smoke since earlier in the week, with visibility reduced and an orange haze over landmarks such as the Empire State Building.
  • A large share of the fires — particularly a cluster in northern/northwestern Ontario — were burning out of control and generating the plumes reaching the US.
  • Health officials advised people to stay indoors, close windows, limit outdoor activity and use N95/KN95-grade masks and air purifiers.
  • Experts attribute increasingly severe wildfire seasons in part to human-caused climate change producing hotter, drier conditions.
  • Fine particulate matter from wildfire smoke poses elevated health risks, especially for children, older adults, pregnant people and those with heart or lung conditions.
  • Organizers of the World Cup final in East Rutherford/New Jersey said they were monitoring the smoke situation ahead of the weekend match.
  • Detroit's AQI was reported at a hazardous level well above 400 on Friday morning (BBC and NPR cite 435).
