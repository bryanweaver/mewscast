Consensus Facts

  • U.S. and Nigerian forces carried out a joint operation that killed a senior Islamic State leader in Nigeria, as announced by both President Trump and Nigerian President Bola Tinubu.
  • The target was identified as Abu Bakr al-Mainuki (also rendered Abu-Bilal al-Minuki), described by Trump as the second-in-command of ISIS globally.
  • Trump announced the operation via a late-night social media post on Truth Social.
  • Nigerian President Tinubu confirmed al-Minuki was killed alongside several of his lieutenants during a strike on his compound in the Lake Chad Basin.
  • Al-Minuki was designated a Specially Designated Global Terrorist by the U.S. in 2023.
  • Al-Minuki was viewed as a key figure in IS organizing and finance and had been plotting attacks against the United States and its interests, according to an unnamed U.S. official.
  • Al-Minuki was associated with the Islamic State West Africa Province (ISWAP) and had links to the broader Boko Haram insurgency.
  • The U.S. had previously sent troops and drones to Nigeria in early 2026 to help advise Nigerian military forces.
  • No U.S. service members were harmed in the operation.
