Consensus Facts

  • Tropical Storm Bertha formed Monday, July 20-21, 2026, in the Gulf of Mexico, upgraded from Tropical Depression Two.
  • Bertha is the second named storm of the 2026 Atlantic hurricane season, following Tropical Storm Arthur.
  • As of the evening advisory, the storm was located about 110 miles south of Panama City, Florida (Axios reports about 105 miles as of 4pm Monday).
  • Bertha had maximum sustained winds of about 40 mph and was moving slowly at about 3 mph in a west-northwest direction.
  • The National Hurricane Center said additional strengthening is possible as the storm approaches land.
  • A tropical storm watch was in effect for a stretch of the Gulf Coast from the Ochlockonee River in Florida westward toward southeastern Louisiana.
  • A storm surge watch was in effect from the mouth of the Mississippi River to the Alabama/Florida border, with possible surge of up to 4 feet.
  • Forecasters projected rainfall of 2 to 4 inches, with locally higher totals up to 8 inches, along the Gulf Coast through Friday.
  • Rainfall could cause flash flooding, especially in urban areas.
  • The storm is forecast to move near or along the northern Gulf Coast for several days, tracking west toward Louisiana and Texas.
  • Tropical Storm Arthur, the season's first named storm, made landfall in June along the Gulf Coast of Texas.
