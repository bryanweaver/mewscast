Consensus Facts

  • Rebecca Bennett, a former healthcare executive and US Navy veteran, won the Democratic primary for New Jersey's 7th Congressional District and will face Republican incumbent Tom Kean Jr, who ran unopposed.
  • Tom Kean Jr has been absent from Congress since early March due to an undisclosed medical condition, making the NJ-7 race more competitive for Democrats.
  • Adam Hamawy, a retired US Army combat surgeon and sharp Israel critic, won the Democratic primary for New Jersey's 12th Congressional District, a solidly blue seat held by retiring Rep. Bonnie Watson Coleman.
  • Hamawy is Muslim, immigrated to the US from Egypt as a baby, and would be the first Muslim lawmaker to represent New Jersey nationally if elected.
  • Hamawy was endorsed by Sen. Bernie Sanders and several progressive 'Squad' members, and was boosted by a pro-Palestinian super PAC called American Priorities that spent more than $1.5 million supporting his campaign.
  • Hamawy supports Medicare for All, the abolition of ICE, and an arms embargo and sanctions on Israel.
  • Josh Turek, a state legislator born with spina bifida from his father's exposure to Agent Orange in Vietnam and a Paralympic gold medalist in wheelchair basketball, won the Iowa Democratic primary for US Senate over Zach Wahls.
  • Turek ran on a 'prairie populist' message and will face Republican Ashley Hinson, a former broadcaster, in the general election for the seat left open by retiring Sen. Joni Ernst.
  • After Turek's win, the Cook Political Report moved the Iowa Senate race from 'likely Republican' to 'lean Republican.'
  • Rob Sand, Iowa's state auditor and the last Democrat in statewide office in the state, won the Democratic nomination for governor.
  • Scott Wiener, a California state senator, advanced to the November runoff in the San Francisco congressional district long held by retiring former House Speaker Nancy Pelosi.
  • The California governor's race remained tight between Republican Steve Hilton, a former Fox News host endorsed by Trump, and Democrat Xavier Becerra, a former Biden administration health secretary.
