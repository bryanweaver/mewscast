Consensus Facts

  • Vance Boelter, 59, was sentenced in federal court in Minneapolis for the killing of Minnesota House Speaker Melissa Hortman and her husband Mark Hortman.
  • Boelter pleaded guilty in June, roughly a year after the June 14, 2025 attacks, in exchange for prosecutors not seeking the death penalty.
  • The sentence was two consecutive life terms plus 40 years.
  • Boelter also critically wounded state Sen. John Hoffman and his wife Yvette in the attacks.
  • Boelter went to Melissa Hortman's home around 3:30 a.m. on June 14, 2025, wearing a tactical uniform and mask and disguised as a police officer.
  • Boelter admitted he spent months identifying and stalking his targets before driving to their homes with the intention of killing them.
  • The attacks sparked the largest police search in Minnesota state history.
  • The Hortman family's golden retriever was shot in the attack and did not survive.
  • Federal defender Manny Atwal represented Boelter in the sentencing proceedings.
