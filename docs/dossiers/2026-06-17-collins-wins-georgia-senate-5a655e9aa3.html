Consensus Facts

  • Rep. Mike Collins won the Georgia Republican Senate runoff on June 16, 2026, defeating former college football coach Derek Dooley.
  • Collins will face incumbent Democratic Sen. Jon Ossoff in the November 2026 general election.
  • President Trump endorsed Collins shortly before the runoff — approximately two days prior.
  • Gov. Brian Kemp endorsed Derek Dooley in the Senate race.
  • The runoff was triggered because no candidate secured 50% of the vote in the May 19 primary.
  • Ossoff is considered the most vulnerable Senate Democrat this cycle, as he is the only Senate Democrat seeking reelection in a state Trump won in 2024.
  • Democrats need a net gain of four Senate seats to take control of the chamber.
  • In the separate governor's runoff, billionaire healthcare executive Rick Jackson defeated Trump-endorsed Lt. Gov. Burt Jones.
  • Rick Jackson spent approximately $100 million of his own money on the governor's race.
  • Jackson will face Democratic former Atlanta Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms in the general election for governor.
  • Collins is a second-term congressman first elected to the House in 2022.
  • Collins touted his authorship of the Laken Riley Act, the first bill Trump signed in his second term.
  • Ossoff holds a significant fundraising advantage heading into the general election.
  • Trump won Georgia by about 2 percentage points in 2024.
  • The Trump-Kemp feud dates back to Kemp's refusal to overturn the 2020 election results in Georgia.
