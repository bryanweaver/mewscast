Consensus Facts

  • Russia attacked Kyiv overnight into Sunday, July 19, 2026, with ballistic missiles and other weapons.
  • The attack began around 1:30 a.m. local time and continued for several hours, with explosions echoing across the capital.
  • One person was killed in the Kyiv attack.
  • Russia launched 41 missiles and 125 drones across Ukraine overnight, according to the Ukrainian air force (per ABC News and CBS News).
  • President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said most of the missiles targeted the capital.
  • The strikes sparked fires in multiple Kyiv districts, damaging residential buildings, office and industrial sites, a dormitory, and vehicles, per Ukraine's State Emergency Service.
  • The attack again highlighted Ukraine's shortage of U.S.-made Patriot air defense systems, its most effective means of intercepting ballistic missiles.
  • U.S. President Trump has said he is prepared to grant Ukraine licenses to produce Patriot interceptor missiles, though details and timeline remain unclear (per ABC News and CBS News).
