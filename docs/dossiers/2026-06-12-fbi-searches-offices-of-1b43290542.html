Consensus Facts

  • FBI agents executed a search warrant at the Cleveland office of the Ohio Organizing Collaborative on Thursday, June 12, 2026, seizing documents and computer files.
  • The Ohio Organizing Collaborative is a grassroots organization that works on voter registration, criminal justice reform, and racial justice.
  • Prentiss Haney, a board member of the Ohio Organizing Collaborative, served as the organization's public spokesperson regarding the search.
  • Federal agents also went to the homes of people affiliated with the organization seeking interviews and information about alleged voter fraud.
  • Haney accused the agents of intimidation tactics and harassment and expressed concern the investigation was designed to sow doubt in coming elections.
  • A person familiar with the matter said investigators were examining potential fraud violations.
  • The FBI and Justice Department declined to comment on the specifics of the investigation.
  • To obtain a search warrant, federal authorities must convince a judge that probable cause of criminal activity exists.
  • The search comes in a state expected to have hotly contested races this fall for governor and U.S. Senate.
