Consensus Facts

  • The U.S. Supreme Court ruled 5-4 in favor of Terry Pitchford, a Black man on death row in Mississippi, who claimed racial bias in the makeup of the jury that convicted him.
  • Justice Brett Kavanaugh wrote the majority opinion, joined by Chief Justice John Roberts and the court's three liberal justices.
  • Justices Clarence Thomas, Samuel Alito, Neil Gorsuch, and Amy Coney Barrett dissented.
  • Pitchford was convicted of capital murder and sentenced to death for his role in a 2004 grocery store robbery in which the store owner was killed.
  • Pitchford was 18 at the time of the crime; the other teenager involved, Eric Bullins, fired the fatal shots but was ineligible for the death penalty because he was 16.
  • During jury selection, then-District Attorney Doug Evans used peremptory strikes to remove four of five prospective Black jurors.
  • Pitchford's defense lawyers objected to the strikes under Batson v. Kentucky, the 1986 Supreme Court precedent barring race-based exclusion of jurors.
  • The trial jury was composed of 11 white jurors and one Black juror.
  • Doug Evans was also the prosecutor in the Curtis Flowers case, in which the Supreme Court overturned Flowers's conviction approximately seven years ago, citing Evans's pattern of striking Black jurors.
  • Kavanaugh wrote that 'things broke down' in the jury selection process and that the trial court did not afford Pitchford's counsel sufficient opportunity to rebut the prosecutor's proffered race-neutral reasons for striking the Black jurors.
  • The state may seek to retry Pitchford.
  • Doug Evans is now retired.
