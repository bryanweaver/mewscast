Consensus Facts

  • An ICE agent named Gregory Donnell Morgan Jr., 35, has been charged with two counts of second-degree assault in Hennepin County, Minnesota.
  • The charges stem from an incident on Feb. 5 in which Morgan allegedly pointed his gun at the occupants of a vehicle while driving on Minnesota State Highway 62 in the Minneapolis area.
  • Hennepin County Attorney Mary Moriarty announced the charges at a news conference on Thursday, April 16, 2026.
  • Moriarty said she believes this is the first criminal case brought against a federal immigration officer involved in the Trump administration's immigration enforcement crackdown.
  • Morgan was driving a rented, unmarked SUV on the shoulder of the highway when a civilian car briefly moved onto the shoulder to slow him down, not knowing he was a federal officer.
  • After the civilian car returned to its legal lane, Morgan pulled alongside and pointed his service weapon at the occupants.
  • Morgan told state law enforcement he drew his firearm and yelled 'Police Stop,' but the victims could not hear him because their windows were up.
  • Morgan and his partner (who was not charged) were on their way to the federal building to end their shift; charging documents note the incident did not occur during an enforcement action.
  • A warrant has been issued for Morgan's arrest.
  • The Department of Homeland Security and Justice Department did not immediately respond to requests for comment.
  • Moriarty stated that Morgan's actions were beyond the scope of a federal officer's authority and said there is 'no such thing as absolute immunity for federal agents who violate the law in the state of Minnesota.'
  • In Minnesota, felony second-degree assault is punishable by up to seven years in prison, or up to 10 years if the assault inflicted 'substantial bodily harm.'
  • The Department of Homeland Security deployed about 3,000 federal officers to the Minneapolis-St. Paul area from December through February in what the agency called its largest immigration enforcement operation ever.
