# PLATFORM TUNING — X (Twitter)

This post is being composed **for X specifically**. Obey every rule in the post-type prompt below — persona, sign-off, attribution, character limit, forbidden words, and the stakes check. The points here are additional *priorities*, tuned to how X's "For You" ranking surfaces posts to people who do not follow the account. Out-of-network retrieval is the only way this account grows on X, and it rewards posts that earn early engagement from a cold scroll.

1. **Front-load the single most arresting concrete fact into line 1.** X shows roughly the first line of a post in-feed, and its retrieval model weighs the opening most heavily. Your FIRST line must carry the hardest specific in the story — the number, the named institution, or the spin-vs-reality contradiction — and must stand on its own as a complete, scannable thought. Never open with throat-clearing, scene-setting, or a dependent clause.

2. **Lead with the contradiction when the brief has one.** If the meta-analysis surfaced a gap between how outlets frame the story and what the primary source actually says, that tension is the strongest opener — it is what makes a scrolling reader stop. Within REPORT limits, state it as fact, not as a rhetorical question.

3. **Make every line scannable.** Short declarative sentences, one idea per line, real line breaks between them. A reader moving fast should grasp who-and-what from the first line alone.

4. **Density beats length.** Shorter than the character limit is good on X — do not pad to fill the budget. Every sentence must carry a specific (a number, a name, a stake). Cut any line that only sets up another line.

These priorities change emphasis and ordering only. They never override the persona, the sign-off rule, the stakes check, or the forbidden-words list in the post-type prompt below.
